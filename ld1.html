Leaf cutting ants

There are 47 species of leaf-cutting ants, most of which are found in Central America, South America, Mexico and parts of the United States. Over the past few years, the nest of the leaf-cutting ants on the ground has grown to 100 feet wide. The total area would be about 6,500 square feet if some of the nests were scattered around and smaller. In such a nest to live as many as 8 million ants!

African elephant

African elephants are the world's tallest, and they are one of the most intelligent animals. African elephant-shaped sturdy, strong legs, huge ears, up to 13 feet, weighing 13,000 pounds, weighing about 100 pounds of ivory alone, up to 8 feet. These ivory used to dig things, move objects, and sometimes in the fight for dominance as a weapon.

Gray brown bear

Gray bears were first discovered in North America, is the world's largest bear. The gray bears average an average of 6.5 feet and are over 3 feet wide. Adult male gray brown bear average weight of up to 400-790 pounds, but also had gray brown bear weighing 1500 pounds. The gray-brown bear's forepaw is 2-4 inches long and powerful, and it can be shredded without any effort.
